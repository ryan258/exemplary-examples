# Discovery Simulations (Synthetic Dry-Run)

## What this directory is

Every file in `discovery/simulations/` is **synthetic**. The interview records here
were generated by a model role-playing fictional prospective customers. Nobody was
interviewed. No real person, name, handle, email, employer, or profile is represented.
The "P1".."P6" identifiers and any first names are deliberately generic placeholders.

## Why it exists

Real customer-discovery interviews are **blocked** at stage P0.0: there is no approved
contact system, no consent/note-taking procedure signed off by the Accountable Principal,
and no real recruited participants (see `discovery/RECRUITMENT-PLAN.md`). Rather than sit
idle, the team is using this synthetic set to **dry-run the process**:

- exercise the 7-part interview guide end to end;
- practice filling the per-interview record template;
- practice the cross-interview synthesis table;
- and — most importantly — surface problems in the **interview guide itself**
  (see `INTERVIEW-GUIDE-FRICTION-LOG.md`), which is the real deliverable of a dry run.

## What this directory is NOT

- It is **not evidence** of market demand, willingness to pay, or design-partner interest.
- A synthetic interview is **weak evidence by construction**: the "participant" cannot
  surprise the researcher with facts the researcher did not already imagine, cannot
  supply real recent behavior, and cannot make a real commitment. Any signal here is
  a rehearsal artifact, not a market reading.
- It must **never** feed a real Checkpoint / proceed–stop decision.
- It must **never** be published, quoted externally, or shown to a client, partner, or
  prospect as though it were real research.
- It must **never** be merged into or counted alongside `discovery/INTERVIEW-RECORD.md`
  outputs or `discovery/SYNTHESIS.md` once real interviews exist.
