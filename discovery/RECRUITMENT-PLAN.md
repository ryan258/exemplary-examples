## Screening rule

Include a participant when all are true:

- they personally created, reviewed, commissioned, or relied on business examples/exhibits in the last six months;
- the work supported a decision, pitch, investment, or executive communication;
- they can describe actual behavior rather than only react to a hypothetical offer;
- they fit one of the target segments and improve sample balance.

Exclude or deprioritize when:

- their only connection is enthusiasm for satire or AI;
- they cannot speak to a recent workflow;
- participation would create a conflict, pressure, or confidentiality concern;
- the sample already overrepresents their segment or relationship type.

## Neutral invitation draft — principal approval required

> Exemplary Examples is researching how consultants, agencies, and presentation professionals find, model, verify, and present business examples under deadline pressure. We are inviting a small number of 30-minute research conversations about recent workflow, tradeoffs, and trust expectations. This is research, not a sales call. We will not ask for confidential client details. With your permission, we will take written notes and de-identify findings. Participation is optional, and you may request an accessible format or stop at any time.

Do not add the satirical brand explanation or pilot pitch to the invitation. The interview guide introduces those only after current behavior is understood.

## Yield and stopping rules

- Begin with 12 approved candidates plus 4 alternates.
- Do not claim a response or conversion rate before outreach evidence exists.
- Review sample balance after interviews 3 and 5.
- Stop at five only if at least two segments are represented, repeated recent behavior is clear, and two plausible design partners accept a concrete follow-up.
- Continue to eight when the pattern, buyer, delivery model, or trust response remains mixed.
- Revise or stop if participants like the idea but do not evidence the underlying problem or willingness to act.

## Evidence to record

Record only de-identified findings in the repository using `INTERVIEW-RECORD.md`. Keep contact/follow-up state in the future approved contact system. Update `SYNTHESIS.md` after interviews 3, 5, and 8 without turning memorable remarks into stronger evidence than the sample supports.
