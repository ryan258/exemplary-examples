# P0.0 Customer Discovery

## Objective

Determine whether consultants, agency staff, and presentation professionals experience the target problem, trust the satirical positioning, accept the professional disclosure boundary, and would consider a limited Executive Retainer pilot.

P0.0 is complete only when:

- At least five target-audience interviews are recorded.
- Each record covers the problem, current alternative, trust response, and disclosure response.
- The findings are synthesized by evidence strength rather than memorable quotations.
- Two or three prospective design partners have agreed to consider the pilot.
- The offer and public language reflect findings rather than internal assumptions.

## Files

- [`INTERVIEW-GUIDE.md`](INTERVIEW-GUIDE.md) — moderator script and question sequence.
- [`INTERVIEW-RECORD.md`](INTERVIEW-RECORD.md) — duplicate once per conversation.
- [`SYNTHESIS.md`](SYNTHESIS.md) — cross-interview evidence and decision record.
- [`RECRUITMENT-PLAN.md`](RECRUITMENT-PLAN.md) — balanced segment plan, screening rule, neutral invitation, and outreach gate.

Store completed interview records in `discovery/interviews/` using non-identifying filenames such as `interview-01.md`. Do not record confidential client names, projects, or data. Obtain permission before recording audio or video; written notes are the default.

## Participant mix

Aim for 5–8 conversations across at least two of these groups:

- Independent or boutique consultants.
- Agency strategy, research, or client-service professionals.
- Presentation, narrative, or executive-communications specialists.
- Internal strategy professionals who regularly commission external support.

Avoid filling the sample entirely with friends, people already enthusiastic about the brand, or people without influence over purchasing or delivery.

## Execution sequence

1. Recruit participants using a neutral description: research into how professionals source, model, and present examples under deadline pressure.
2. Conduct the problem interview before showing the brand or offer.
3. Show the brand concept and professional truth standard only after current behavior is understood.
4. Record evidence immediately after the conversation.
5. Update the synthesis after interviews 3, 5, and 8.
6. Stop at five only if the primary pattern is clear and the participant mix is adequate; otherwise continue to eight.
7. Ask strong-fit participants whether they will consider a design-partner pilot. Interest is not acceptance and must not be overstated.

## Decision rule

Proceed to offer design when:

- The target problem is evidenced by repeated recent behavior, not hypothetical enthusiasm.
- At least three participants describe a material cost, delay, risk, or quality problem.
- The disclosure boundary increases or preserves trust for the intended buyers.
- At least two plausible design partners agree to a concrete follow-up.

Revise or narrow the offer if the problem exists but the retainer, buyer, positioning, or delivery model does not fit. Stop if the only positive signal is politeness toward the idea.
