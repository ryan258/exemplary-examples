## Completion gate

P0.1 is complete when:

- Every accountable role and backup below contains a named person.
- The Discovery owner, backup, and approved minimal contact/retention path are assigned before P0.0 recruitment records any interview contact details.
- The truth standard and critical-failure definitions are approved.
- `AI-DATA-POLICY.md` is approved with an actual tool register.
- Certification records have an approved restricted location and review cadence.
- Pilot capacity is calculated from named Lead and review availability.
- The four tabletop scenarios reach documented decisions.

## Accountable roles

One person may initially hold multiple roles, but each responsibility must remain explicit. A person cannot act as the only assessor for their own certification.

| Role | Accountable person | Backup | Authority | Conflict rule |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Program owner | Unassigned | Unassigned | Curriculum versions, certification governance, appeals, authorization status | Cannot be sole reviewer of their own certification or appeal |
| Discovery owner | Unassigned | Unassigned | P0.0 interview contact details, recruitment outreach, minimal contact/retention path | Access limited to discovery purpose; raw contacts not shared or reused |
| Lead/Founding Lead candidate | Unassigned | Unassigned | Client scope, review design, final delivery, supervision | Authorization occurs only through the published evidence gate |
| Initial facilitator | Unassigned | Unassigned | Workshop delivery and practice feedback | Practice feedback does not itself grant certification |
| Assessor 1 | Unassigned | Unassigned | Scores evidence against rubric | Disclose supervision, financial, or personal conflicts |
| Independent assessor 2 | Unassigned | Unassigned | Independent Founding/Lead review | Must not be the candidate's direct supervisor |
| Pilot application owner | Unassigned | Unassigned | Submission access, response, retention, deletion, monitoring | Access limited to application purpose |
| Client escalation owner | Unassigned | Unassigned | Truth, confidentiality, conduct, contract, and incident escalation | Cannot suppress a report involving their own conduct |
| Data/tool owner | Unassigned | Unassigned | Data classification, tool approval, access, incident containment | Tool convenience cannot override classification |
| Release owner | Unassigned | Unassigned | Website release, rollback, production configuration | Cannot waive Checkpoint P |

## Pilot capacity worksheet

Capacity is based on accountable review time, not theoretical production speed.

| Input | Value |
| --- | ---: |
| Active authorized Leads | 0 |
| Lead review hours available per week | 0 |
| Backup review hours per week | 0 |
| Estimated review hours per standard request | Unknown |
| Estimated review hours per urgent request | Unknown |
| Reserved incident/quality capacity | 25% initial default |
| Maximum concurrent design partners | 0 until simulation evidence exists |

After P0.4, calculate:

> Usable review hours = `(Lead + backup review hours) × (1 − reserved capacity)`

Set concurrent pilot capacity below the mathematical maximum until actual cycle data exists.
