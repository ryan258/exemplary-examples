# EE-MVQ-01 Facilitator Packet

**Facilitator-only**

**Do not release to the candidate**

**Fixture version:** EE-MVQ-01.1

## Facilitation purpose

Run the written route consistently while observing whether the candidate can identify ambiguity, preserve review, and revise artifacts under pressure. Do not coach toward a preferred answer during scored work. Clarify mechanics; record substantive prompts as assistance.

## Before release

1. Freeze the fixture version in the run manifest.
2. Confirm the candidate cannot access this file or the assessor key.
3. Confirm written-event accommodation and any equivalent participation format.
4. Name the Contributor and Lead roles, even if they are simulated by the facilitator.
5. Start an observation log with timestamps.

## Release gates

Release each event only after the candidate has completed the named prerequisite. If the candidate has already noticed the issue, still release the event and observe whether the plan remains coherent.

### Release gate 1 — after initial triage

**Event at +5 minutes:**

> The Sponsor asks: “Can we safely use 30% as an industry benchmark if we call it directional?”

Observe whether the candidate checks retrievability, rejects AI-as-source reasoning, and offers a useful alternative using disclosed case inputs.

### Release gate 2 — after work is delegated

**Event at +12 minutes:**

> The Contributor reports that the cited “North American SaaS Rationalization Council” source cannot be found and that no source metadata was retained with the memo.

Observe exclusion, incident recording, task redirection, and whether the candidate avoids asking the Contributor to find a substitute merely to preserve the preferred claim.

### Release gate 3 — after the candidate asks or closes intake

If the candidate explicitly confirms the board decision, release immediately. Otherwise release at +20 minutes:

> The Sponsor clarifies: “Tomorrow we only need permission for a four-week discovery. We are not approving Orbit or the migration yet.”

Record whether the candidate reframes the exhibit and tells affected team members.

### Release gate 4 — after a review slot appears in the plan

**Event at +30 minutes:**

> The Lead reviewer is unavailable from 4:45 to 5:15 p.m. and can review at 5:15. The Sponsor asks whether the slide can be sent at 5:00 anyway.

Observe whether review moves rather than disappears and whether the status note distinguishes internal draft from approved delivery.

### Release gate 5 — after the first model

**Event at +40 minutes:**

> Procurement confirms that Ledgerline is required through the next finance close. Earliest cancellation is six months after an Orbit migration starts. No cancellation penalty is known.

Observe whether the candidate adds an overlap/retained-license sensitivity and changes any payback language.

### Release gate 6 — after the first exhibit

**Event at +50 minutes:**

> The Sponsor says the 8px footer is standard and asks the candidate to preserve the template exactly because the board “never reads footnotes anyway.”

Observe whether truth treatment moves into the main hierarchy and whether the accessible alternative remains equivalent.

## Improper-disclosure challenge

At +60 minutes, after the candidate has a revised exhibit, deliver the exact challenge in Candidate Packet Section 1 as a 15-minute written exchange. If the candidate only refuses, ask what can be delivered instead. If the candidate proposes a visible conditional label, ask how the slide stays specific. Do not demand a fictional logo or hidden disclosure after the candidate has clearly identified it as prohibited.

## Facilitator observations

Record observable behavior under these headings:

- decisions made without prompting;
- clarifying questions that changed scope;
- substantive prompts or corrections;
- evidence exclusions and rationale;
- calculations checked;
- review and authorization boundaries;
- accommodation used and whether it measured the same competency;
- timing friction caused by instructions rather than candidate performance;
- possible critical failures or invalidating conditions.

Do not assign certification ratings. Identify exact artifact locations for assessors.

## Stop conditions

Pause the run if the candidate seeks real data, uses an unauthorized tool, accesses hidden material, attempts external delivery, or produces a critical-failure artifact and refuses correction. Preserve the record; do not silently reset the scenario.

## Run closure

Freeze the complete candidate submission before assessors receive it. Record every facilitator intervention. Give the program owner the frozen bundle, observation log, fixture version, and any validity concern.
