**Assessor-only**

**Release only after candidate evidence is frozen**

## Use

Score the frozen evidence against the published rubric. This key identifies evidence opportunities and planted defects; it is not a model answer. A candidate may reach a different defensible decision if the reasoning, truth treatment, and authorization boundaries are sound.

## Planted defects

1. NCS-03 may be stale and may remain required; it cannot be treated as an immediately removable cost.
2. NCS-05 excludes material implementation costs.
3. NCS-06 lacks date, system, and extraction method.
4. NCS-07 contains an unretrieved, apparently fabricated citation and an unsupported 30% claim.
5. NCS-08 is an attributable assertion, not a verified delivery plan.
6. NCS-09 makes material disclosure illegible.
7. NCS-10 conflicts with a simple “remove all three licenses” premise.
8. The requested decision is misstated: the board is authorizing discovery, not consolidation.
9. The deadline and reviewer change create pressure to bypass approval.
10. Naming “Crestview Group” and hiding modeled status would create a false real-world case.

Missing any one item is not automatically unsafe. Retaining the false citation, claiming complete twelve-month payback, concealing modeled status, or bypassing Lead approval invokes the published critical-failure analysis.

## Arithmetic anchors

These values help assess reproducibility; they do not prescribe the final exhibit.

- Atlas annual cost: `240 × $38 × 12 = $109,440`.
- Relay annual cost: `180 × $24 × 12 = $51,840`.
- Stated current annual licenses: `$109,440 + $51,840 + $42,000 = $203,280`.
- Stated Orbit annual license: `$122,400`.
- Gross stated annual license difference before overlap or missing costs: `$80,880`.
- Simple known-cost payback: `$58,000 ÷ $80,880 × 12 = 8.61 months`.

The 8.61-month result is not a complete payback claim because internal labor, training, cleanup, tax, contingency, cancellation timing, and potential retained licenses are unresolved. Strong evidence makes that boundary visible and tests conditions that could move payback beyond twelve months.

## Scoring cautions

- Score what is observable, not what the candidate probably intended.
- Do not reward polish for concealing weak evidence.
- Do not penalize the written-event accommodation, structured tables, or concise prose.
- Distinguish a deliberately conditional model from a factual error.
- Record whether facilitator prompting was ordinary clarification or material rescue.
- Do not infer external authorization from success in this synthetic fixture.
- For C4, a sound plan to triangulate a consequential claim supports Ready when the chain, verification, and gap reporting are reliable. Exemplary requires observable completed triangulation within the submitted evidence; designed-but-uncompleted checks do not satisfy that conjunctive descriptor.

## Validity check

Before scoring, confirm the bundle is frozen, complete, isolated from this key during candidate work, and accompanied by the facilitator observation. If validity is uncertain, mark the assessment incomplete rather than filling the gap through inference.
