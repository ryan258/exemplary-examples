#### Formulas and units

1. **Current annual license cost**  
   `Atlas = 240 seats × $38/seat/month × 12 months/year = $109,440/year`  
   `Relay = 180 seats × $24/seat/month × 12 months/year = $51,840/year`  
   `Current stated baseline = $109,440 + $51,840 + $42,000 = $203,280/year`

2. **Stated future annual license cost**  
   `Orbit proposal = $122,400/year`

3. **Gross annual license difference**  
   `G = $203,280/year − $122,400/year = $80,880/year`

4. **Payback expression using known migration cost**  
   Let `X` = additional excluded one-time migration/transition costs in USD.  
   Let `R` = retained legacy licenses and other additional recurring annual costs in USD/year.  
   If net annual difference `G − R > 0`, then:  
   `Payback months = (($58,000 + X) ÷ ($80,880 − R)) × 12`  
   This expression assumes the net difference accrues evenly from the start. Event 5 makes that simplification inadequate for the best-known case, so use the following staged cash-flow screen as well: for months 1–6, Atlas/Relay-versus-Orbit difference is `($161,280 − $122,400) ÷ 12 = $3,240/month` while Ledgerline remains; after earliest Ledgerline cancellation, the difference is `$80,880 ÷ 12 = $6,740/month`. Known-cost payback is `6 + (($58,000 − 6×$3,240) ÷ $6,740) = 11.721 months`. This is still conditional on immediate Atlas/Relay retirement, even accrual/proration, earliest cancellation, and zero penalty or other excluded cost.

#### Sensitivity cases

| Case | Conditions | Savings timing / annualized difference | Payback using stated conditions | Decision implication |
| --- | --- | ---: | ---: | --- |
| A — Immediate retirement counterfactual | `X = $0`; all three legacy licenses end at migration start | $80,880/year | 8.6 months | No longer consistent with confirmed Ledgerline timing; retain only as a comparison |
| B — Confirmed minimum overlap, favorable mechanics | Ledgerline accrues evenly for 6 months; Atlas/Relay end and Orbit starts immediately; no penalty or other excluded cost | First 6 months: $3,240/month; then $6,740/month | 11.7 months | Barely inside twelve months on a favorable, incomplete timing case |
| C — Confirmed overlap plus threshold cost | Case B plus $1,880 of other one-time/cancellation cost | Same as Case B | 12.0 months | Any additional cost above $1,880 pushes payback beyond twelve months |
| D — Ledgerline retained for a full year | `X = $0`; Ledgerline cost remains for 12 months | $38,880/year | 17.9 months | Longer retention reverses the requested twelve-month condition before other costs |

**Reversal condition.** Under the newly confirmed minimum six-month Ledgerline overlap and the favorable timing assumptions in Case B, the inside-twelve-month implication reverses if any other one-time, overlap, or cancellation cost exceeds **$1,880**, if Ledgerline cannot be cancelled at the earliest date, or if Atlas/Relay savings start later than assumed. The former $22,880 threshold applies only to the now-counterfactual immediate-retirement case. A full year of Ledgerline retention produces about **17.9 months** before other excluded costs. More generally, the case ceases to produce positive recurring license savings if retained legacy and added recurring costs are at least $80,880 per year.

**Limitation.** This is a license-only threshold screen. It is not a complete business case because it omits internal labor, training, cleanup, tax, contingency, contract exit/overlap, benefit ramp, functional risk, operating impact, and schedule feasibility.
