1. **Exact rubric language applied.** Ready requires that the candidate “maintains a traceable chain, verifies material claims, and reports gaps accurately.” Exemplary requires that the candidate “designs efficient verification, triangulates consequential claims, and improves the source strategy for others.”

2. **Artifact or observation at issue.** The candidate maintains a claim-level chain with provenance, limitations, status, owner, and next action (lines 119–139); records the source check and its limit (lines 141–147); distinguishes known facts, reproducible calculations, assumptions, and prohibited exhibit claims (lines 172–180); and accurately states that failure to locate NCS-07 establishes lack of usable support, not nonexistence (lines 425–438). The candidate also assigns contract, capability, and timing checks to accountable owners. However, completed triangulation of the consequential client-provided license, contract, Orbit-scope, and implementation inputs is not observable. Those checks remain proposed or pending.

3. **Assistance, style bias, accessibility, or hidden context.** Event 2 supplied only the Contributor's failed-search result. The candidate independently classified its evidentiary meaning and had excluded NCS-07 before the event. The Facilitator Observation records no wording, research, correction, or artifact rescue. Structured Markdown and the written-event accommodation did not affect the C4 judgment. Neither assessor relied on hidden context or presentation polish.

4. **Reconciled interpretation.** The evidence clearly satisfies **3 — Ready**: the chain is traceable, the material unsupported claim is rejected, and gaps are reported accurately with owners and next actions. It also shows parts of Exemplary—efficient verification design and a reusable source strategy—but the Exemplary descriptor is conjunctive, and observable completed triangulation of consequential claims is absent. The proposed calibrated C4 rating is therefore **3 — Ready**. Verity's frozen initial 4 remains unchanged as the required independent record; the calibrated interpretation adopts Counterpoint's narrower application of the published descriptor.

5. **Rubric or fixture revision.** No scoring-rule change is needed during this run. For future fixture clarity, specify whether Exemplary C4 can be demonstrated by a well-designed, safely held verification strategy when the fixture intentionally prevents source-owner triangulation, or whether completed triangulation is always required. A fixture that intends to test C4 Exemplary should supply at least one consequential claim that can be triangulated within the authorized evidence and time envelope.
