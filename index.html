Exhibit 2.0 · The premise

The joke is recognizable.
The boundary is real.

Business evidence has a peculiar habit of arriving after the conclusion. Our satire points at that habit. Our professional work refuses to participate in it.

Satirical fiction belongs inside the clearly disclosed brand context. Client work distinguishes sourced facts, client-provided facts, calculations, inferences, and modeled scenarios. If a reasonable audience could confuse constructed material with a verified event, we change it before delivery.

We can make an example clearer, more useful, and more persuasive. We cannot make it true by making it beautiful.

Exhibit 4.0 · Method, not mythology

Proof begins with the work.

There are no client logos, anonymous accolades, or imaginary performance statistics here. The early proof is an inspectable method.

  1. 01

    Frame

    Name the decision, audience, alternatives, consequence, and evidence that could change the answer.

  2. 02

    Classify

    Separate sourced fact, supplied fact, calculation, inference, modeled scenario, and satirical fiction.

  3. 03

    Construct

    Build only what the decision needs, with assumptions and limitations visible in the working.

  4. 04

    Challenge

    Use independent agent review to look for unsupported claims, brittle logic, and misleading presentation.

  5. 05

    Deliver

    Release within authorization, with an Evidence Register and the appropriate accountable review.

  6. 06

    Learn

    Measure decision usefulness, defects, rescue work, and cycle time; revise the system from evidence.
